# Literature Database

This document synthesizes the papers from your literature table, categorized by their primary themes to help you quickly find evidence for your claims.

## 1. Football Tactical & Tracking Analytics
These papers establish that tactical concepts (like spacing and shape) can be measured computationally.
- **Memmert, Lemmink, & Sampaio (2017)**: Foundational review of spatiotemporal tactical analysis; summarizes coordination and compactness.
- **Low et al. (2020)**: Systematic review of 77 studies on collective tactical behaviours using positional data.
- **Andrienko et al. (2021)**: Visual-analytic structures for understanding coordinated movement in football.
- **Goes et al. (2021)**: Quantifying tactical performance from tracking data to predict match outcomes.
- **Ötting & Karlis (2023)**: Using hidden Markov models to classify tactics automatically from tracking data.
- **Kröckel & Bodendorf (2020)**: Applying process-mining to football event logs for tactical insight.

## 2. Advanced AI & Machine Learning in Football
Showcases the current "elite" state of the art that grassroots struggles to adopt.
- **Wang et al. (2024) [TacticAI]**: High-profile Nature Communications paper; built an AI assistant for corner-kick tactics evaluated by Liverpool FC staff.
- **Teixeira et al. (2025)**: Systematic review mapping 32 studies on AI-based tactical behavior and movement patterns.
- **Moya et al. (2025)**: Systematic review of 172 papers on ML applied to professional football for performance and results prediction.
- **Hewitt & Karakuş (2023)**: Machine learning approach for player and position-adjusted Expected Goals (xG).
- **Alves et al. (2025)**: Systematic review of Social Network Analysis in football (passing structures).

## 3. Explainable AI (XAI) & Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)
The theoretical bridge for making AI usable and trustworthy.
- **Kranzinger et al. (2026)**: Scoping review of XAI in sports science, noting heavy reliance on SHAP and limited user validation (Highlights the gap you are filling).
- **Postma et al. (2022)**: Develops a design space and taxonomy for sports interaction technology (HCI).
- **Smith, Scheme, & Bateman (2025)**: Framework for interactive sport training technology—useful for low-resource and coach-friendly design.
- **Camilleri et al. (2025)**: Explores explainable multimodal sensing for swimming analysis (coach-centered design analogue).
- **Schwalbe & Finzel (2024)**: Broad taxonomy of XAI methods and concepts.
- **Wachholz et al. (2025)**: Pilot study on trust and acceptance of AI-generated exercise plans among athletes and coaches.

## 4. Grassroots & Broader Digitalization Context
Evidence that non-elite contexts have different needs.
- **Wang (2025)**: Examines how digital technology supports sustainable development in alumni/non-elite football settings.
- **Hammes et al. (2022)**: Review of AI in elite sports that emphasizes practical barriers like deployment, explainability, and domain constraints.
- **Canzone et al. (2025)**: Narrative review framing AI as an assistive training tool rather than a coach replacement.
