# Research Overview: Explainable AI in Grassroots Sports

## Title
**Explainable AI in Grassroots Sports: Designing Tactical Software for Low-Resource Football Teams**

## Core Problem Statement
Current advancements in artificial intelligence for football (such as TacticAI) are heavily biased toward elite, professional environments. They rely on high-fidelity tracking data, extensive event streams, and technically fluent analysts. 

However, **grassroots and amateur football teams** (which make up the vast majority of the sport) face a completely different set of constraints:
- They lack expensive tracking infrastructure and rely on ordinary video.
- Coaches have limited time and lack data science expertise.
- Current "watered-down" elite tools do not fit their operational realities.

## Research Premise
Grassroots football does not need a diluted copy of elite analytics; it needs tools designed for its own constraints and routines. Explainability (XAI) is not just a "nice-to-have" interface feature—it is a **core design requirement**. Coaches must be able to understand, trust, and act on AI recommendations without needing a data science degree.

## Research Aims
The concept paper outlines four primary aims:
1. **Synthesize** relevant scholarship across football tactical analysis, sports AI, Explainable AI (XAI), and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI).
2. **Scope** the research problem to distinguish *tactical coaching support* from broader personal training and fitness tracking applications.
3. **Propose** a human-centered methodology (HCI) for designing and evaluating a prototype system tailored for low-resource environments.
4. **Articulate** the expected theoretical and practical contributions of this work for grassroots sports.

## Key Theoretical Frameworks
- **HCI (Human-Computer Interaction)**: Ensuring the tool is usable, accessible, and fits into the actual workflow of amateur coaches.
- **XAI (Explainable AI)**: Providing transparent reasoning for why the AI flags certain clips or tactical patterns (e.g., shape, spacing, synchronization) so the coach can trust the system.
- **Collective Tactical Behavior**: Moving beyond isolated player metrics (like speed or distance) to understand team coordination, which is much more valuable for tactical coaching.
