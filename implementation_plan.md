# System Design & Methodology Proposal

This document outlines a concrete, actionable plan for exactly what you should build for your research, how the AI will work, and how you will evaluate it.

## 1. The Exact Deliverable
For this research, you should build a **Functional MVP Web Prototype** paired with a **User Evaluation Study**. 
You are building the **"Grassroots Tactical Assistant" (GTA)**.
- **What it is**: A simple web application where a coach uploads a standard video clip (e.g., from a phone or a basic VEO camera on a tripod).
- **What it does**: It automatically tracks players, calculates basic tactical metrics (like defensive shape), and flags interesting moments.
- **The XAI Hook**: When it flags a moment (e.g., "Poor Defensive Compactness"), it provides a highly visual, easy-to-understand explanation directly on the video.

## 2. The AI & Computer Vision Pipeline (The "How")
You do **not** need to train a massive AI from scratch. You should leverage existing, pre-trained Computer Vision (CV) models available via Hugging Face and Ultralytics.

### Step-by-Step Pipeline:
1. **Player & Ball Detection**: Use **YOLOv8** (or YOLOv10). It is open-source, fast, and highly accurate at drawing bounding boxes around people and sports balls.
2. **Player Tracking**: Use **ByteTrack** or **BoT-SORT**. This assigns an ID to each player so the system knows Player 1 is the same person across frames.
3. **Team Separation**: Use a simple **K-Means Clustering** algorithm on the colors inside the player bounding boxes to automatically group them into Team A, Team B, and Referees.
4. **Tactical Metric Calculation**: Instead of complex 2D pitch mapping (which is hard on amateur video), calculate **relative spatial metrics in the frame**:
   - *Compactness*: Draw a convex hull (a polygon) connecting the outermost players of a team. Calculate the area. If the area suddenly expands, the team has lost its defensive shape.

## 3. Data Strategy: Where to get Data?
You need data for two phases: Development and Evaluation.

- **Development Data (To build the prototype)**: Use the **SoccerNet** dataset. It is an open-source dataset with thousands of hours of football video and pre-labeled tracking data. This is perfect for testing your YOLO models without needing to record your own footage.
- **Evaluation Data (To test with users)**: Go to your university's amateur football team. Ask to record 1-2 of their training matches using a standard tripod camera or a smartphone. This proves your system works on *actual grassroots footage*, which is the core argument of your paper!

## 4. XAI Strategy: How to make it Explainable?
Explainability for a non-technical football coach cannot be SHAP values or feature importance graphs. It must be **Visual and Contextual**.

1. **Visual Overlays (The "Show Me" Explainability)**:
   - When the AI flags a "Defensive Line Break," the UI overlays a colored polygon connecting the defensive players.
   - It draws a bright red line showing the exact distance (gap) between the center-backs that allowed the pass through.
2. **Natural Language Summaries (The "Tell Me" Explainability)**:
   - Use a lightweight LLM (like Llama-3 via API) to translate the raw tracking data into a sentence. 
   - *Example*: Instead of showing "Metric X = 45.2," the UI outputs: *"The AI flagged this clip because the gap between the Left Back and Center Back increased beyond 15 meters, breaking the defensive shape."*
3. **Confidence Scores**: Always show the AI's confidence (e.g., "AI is 85% sure this was a counter-attack opportunity"). This builds trust because the system admits when it is unsure.

## 5. UI / HCI Design Principles
The interface must be designed for an amateur coach holding an iPad on the side of a pitch.
- **The Dashboard**: A standard video player in the center. On the right, a scrollable list of "Tactical Events" generated by the AI.
- **Interactive Toggles**: The coach can click checkboxes to turn the XAI visualizations on or off (e.g., `[x] Show Defensive Polygon`, `[ ] Show Player IDs`).
- **Feedback Loop**: Include a "Was this helpful?" thumbs up/down button next to each AI insight. This is crucial for your HCI evaluation.

## 6. Research Methodology (How to write the paper)
Your paper's methodology should follow this flow:
1. **System Development**: Briefly describe the YOLO/Tracking pipeline.
2. **XAI Interface Design**: Detail the UI overlays and explainability features.
3. **User Evaluation (The Core of the Paper)**: 
   - Recruit 5-10 amateur/grassroots coaches.
   - Show them your prototype processing their own team's footage.
   - Conduct **Think-Aloud Interviews**: Ask them to use the software and verbalize their thoughts.
   - Measure **Trust and Comprehension**: Did the visual overlays help them understand *why* the AI flagged the clip? Did they trust the system more than a "black box" dashboard?
